Our Story
ZIMBABWE'S FIRST ANIME CONVENTION

OTAKUKON is Zimbabwe's first ever anime convention, created by Otaku Konnect — a Zimbabwean organisation dedicated to building an environment where fans of Japanese animation, manga, gaming, and pop culture can share and learn from one another.

We held our first full-day convention in December 2023, following a community watch-day and merch fair earlier that year. Since then, OTAKUKON has grown into the country's premier annual gathering for the anime community.

Our goal is simple: grow the community that appreciates Japanese pop culture within Zimbabwe, and give fans a safe and enjoyable space to belong to.

Got Questions?
FREQUENTLY ASKED
Where exactly is Moto Republik?+
Moto Republik is at 3 Allan Wilson Avenue, Harare CBD. It is accessible by kombi, taxi, and private transport. Parking is available on site.
What is Otaku Konnect?+
Otaku Konnect is the Zimbabwean organisation behind OTAKUKON. Founded to create a space where fans of anime, manga, gaming, and Japanese pop culture can connect and grow together. OTAKUKON is their flagship annual event, first held in December 2023.
Can I buy tickets at the door?+
Yes — but Phase 2 pricing ($5 cosplay / $8 standard) ends August 5. Walk-in prices on the day will be higher, so booking early saves you money.
How do I enter the cosplay championship?+
Purchase a Cosplay Ticket and register your character via WhatsApp (+263 785 552 326). Pre-registration is required — spots are limited. Judging covers craftsmanship, accuracy, and stage performance.
Is there an age limit?+
OTAKUKON is open to all ages. Under-12s must be accompanied by a parent or guardian. It is a family-friendly event.
Can I be a vendor or artist at OTAKUKON?+
Yes — we welcome local artists and sellers. Message us on WhatsApp (+263 785 552 326) or email info@otakukon.com to apply for a vendor stall. Space is limited so apply as early as possible.
How can I volunteer at the event?+
Use the Volunteer Registration card above, or message us directly on WhatsApp. Volunteers are the backbone of OTAKUKON and we are always grateful for people willing to give their time.
